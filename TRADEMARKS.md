# Trademarks & brand usage

## License vs trademark

The OmniTrade source code is licensed under [MIT](LICENSE) — you can
copy, fork, modify, and redistribute the code freely, including for
commercial use. **The MIT license does not grant trademark rights.**

## What's covered

The following are project marks. Even when used on top of an MIT-licensed
fork or derivative, please follow the rules below:

- **The "OmniTrade" name** as a project / product name
- **The compass-mark logo** (`assets/logo.svg`, `assets/logo-horizontal.svg`,
  `assets/favicon.svg`, `assets/logo-mono.svg`)
- **The OmniTrade visual palette** as branding (the specific
  Agno-purple → teal gradient + coral active-spoke combination, when
  used in a way that suggests official affiliation)

## Permitted use (no permission needed)

- **Reference and discussion**: you can say "I run OmniTrade", "I
  forked OmniTrade and changed X", "OmniTrade is faster than my old
  setup" in blog posts, slides, talks, comparisons, reviews,
  documentation, etc.
- **Truthful attribution**: if your project is derived from OmniTrade,
  you can say "Based on OmniTrade" / "Derived from OmniTrade", with a
  link back to https://github.com/gong1414/omnitrade.
- **Fair use of the logo**: you may use the logo unmodified in
  educational materials, conference talks, and articles ABOUT
  OmniTrade. Do not modify, recolor, or distort the mark in ways that
  imply endorsement.

## Restricted use (please ask first)

The following uses can mislead a reasonable observer into thinking
your project / product is the official OmniTrade or is endorsed by it.
Please open an issue for written permission before doing any of these:

- **Naming a fork or derivative "OmniTrade-something"** in a way that
  could be mistaken for the upstream (e.g. "OmniTrade Pro",
  "OmniTrade Cloud", "OmniTrade-as-a-Service")
- **Using the logo as a primary brand element** for a paid product /
  service / SaaS that resells OmniTrade or a fork of it
- **Producing or selling merchandise** (t-shirts, stickers, mugs)
  bearing the OmniTrade name or logo
- **Using the OmniTrade name in a domain name** that could be mistaken
  for an official site (e.g. `omnitrade-official.com`,
  `omnitrade.io`). Domains that clearly disambiguate
  (`omnitrade-fork-myname.com`) are typically fine but please
  link back to upstream.

## Forks: a recommended pattern

If you fork and substantially change OmniTrade for a different
audience, the cleanest path is:

1. Pick a new name for the user-facing product (e.g. "MyAlphaBot")
2. Keep the source license (MIT) intact
3. Keep the README's `🙏 Acknowledgments` section — credit the
   open-source projects OmniTrade was built on
4. Add a one-line attribution at the top of your README:
   `Based on [OmniTrade](https://github.com/gong1414/omnitrade) (MIT).`
5. Replace `assets/logo*.svg` with your own brand assets

This gives you full ownership of your fork's brand while honouring the
MIT terms and giving upstream contributors clear credit.

## Reporting confusion

If you see a fork, product, or domain that uses the OmniTrade name or
logo in a way that could mislead users — especially anything that
suggests official endorsement of paid services or anything that
solicits user funds in OmniTrade's name — please open an issue with
the URL. We don't have lawyers; we just want users not to be confused.

## Questions?

Open an issue. We'd rather have a quick clarifying conversation than
have someone hesitate to use the project.
